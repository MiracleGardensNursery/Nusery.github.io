Admissions and Fees

Our Fees:

Fees include breakfast, lunch, snacks. All fees are due in advance and invoices will be issued mid- month for the following month. Fees are payable during any period of absence from the nursery, including sickness, holidays and during public and bank holidays.

How To Apply

We’re excited you’re interested in learning how to enrol at Miracle Gardens!
⭐The first step to enrolment is to fill out and submit a registration form below.
⭐After receiving your form, you will be invited to attend a tour of our beautiful facilities to review the registration and enrolment process and answer any questions you may have.
⭐If you are happy with what you have seen, we will discuss with you our availability to accept your child for the requested days.
⭐To complete the admission you will be asked to read and sign “Our Terms and Conditions” and to fill in “Child Details Form”

In order to register your child or add you to our waiting list, we will need you to provide the following:
⭐Original Birth certificate for your child
⭐Council Tax Bill (Current year)
⭐Utility bill (within the last three months)
⭐Confirmation letter for FEE2 entitlement (where applicable)
⭐Confirmation letter for 30 hour entitlement (where applicable)
⭐National Insurance Number for mother and father (where relevant), including parent’s date(s) of birth

Miracle Gardens Limited offers a wide range of funding options to families:

Universal Entitlement

All 3 and 4 year olds are eligible for 15 hours per week of free early education. This is a universal entitlement for all children during 38 weeks per year for at least 3 hours, 5 days per week.
The full-time entitlement is an extension of the current entitlement and provides an additional 15 hours of free childcare for children that are eligible.

Working Families Funding (Extended Hours)

Working Families Funding is available for eligible working parents with children aged 9 months to 4 years. A child is entitled to 1,140 funded hours per year, starting from the term after they reach the relevant age and meet the government’s eligibility criteria.
Parents must apply to HMRC through their Childcare Service Account to receive a positive eligibility determination, which provides a valid eligibility code for funded childcare.The code must be given to us before we can claim Early Years Funding. Parents are responsible for revalidating the eligibility code every three months when prompted by HMRC. If this is not completed, the funding may be withdrawn.

Supported Two-Year-Old funding (Expanded Hours)

A child is eligible for 15 hours of funded early education per week (570 per year) starting from the term after they turn two, provided that the child or parent meets the eligibility criteria.
If a child is receiving Supported Two-Year-Old funding, they will continue to have their funded place until they become eligible for Universal Funding, even if they or their parent no longer meet the eligibility requirements during this period.
No food fee is charged when a child is accessing Supported Two-Year-Old funding.

How Miracle Gardens Delivers Early Years Funding:

⭐ At Miracle Gardens, Early Years Funding is delivered over 38 weeks of the year. We provide funded places for eligible children aged 12 to 48 months.
⭐ 15 funded hours are provided through part-time attendance of 5 hours per day over 3 days (from 8:00am to 1:00pm) or 7.5 hours per day over 2 days per week (from 8:00am to 3:30pm).
⭐ 30 funded hours are provided through attendance of 7.5 hours per day over 4 days per week (from 8:00am to 3:30pm). Miracle Gardens requires parents to confirm their childcare booking pattern before the start of each new term.
⭐ Additional fee-paying hours can be provided by parents’ request, subject to our opening hours and availability.
⭐ Miracle Gardens is unable to apply Funded Childcare to any ad-hoc sessions booked outside of the confirmed booking pattern at the beginning of the term.
⭐ Miracle Gardens can make permanent changes to booking patterns in accordance with the nursery Terms and Conditions.
⭐ The entitlement to free childcare through the 15-hour entitlement and 30-hour entitlement relates only to the provision of core childcare services and does not cover the cost of meals, snacks, nappies, wipes, sun cream, other consumables, additional hours, or additional services such as trips.
